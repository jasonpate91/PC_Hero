Motherboard


What is a Motherboard?

A Motherboard is a printed circuit board and foundation of a computer and is the biggest board in the computer chassis. Motherboards are also sometimes refered to as a mb, mainboard, mboard, backplane board, base board, main circuit board, planar board, system board, or a logic board on Apple computers. There are also diffrent types of motherboards, each being designed to fit different types and sizes of computers. Motherboards do not have compatibility with every processor and memory type as each one is designed to work with specific types processors and memory. Motherboards do, however, work with a majority of hard drives regardless of the brand because hard drives are mostly universal. Motherboards also contain various components which include but is not limited to peripherals, interface cards, daughtercards (sound cards, video cards, network cards, hard drives, and other forms of persistent storage), TV tuner cards, extra USB slots, extra FireWire slots, and various other components as well.


Why is a Motherboard important for a computer?

Motherboards are important because they provide an operating location for the computer as well as a secure location for teh CPU and RAM, which are two of the most necessary things in a computer. Motherboards also allocate power and allow communication to and from the CPU, RAM, and other computer hardware components. Motherboards also control the flow of external data through the ports such as the USB and IDE and guides the data to the hard drives. Most parts that help in the function of the computer are either components of a motherboard or are plugged into it.
